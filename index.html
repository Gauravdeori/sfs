The mission at Jonai was initiated in 1979 by Fr. Abraham Kuttiankal, who was then serving as an Assistant Parish Priest at Kumrikatta for one year. He was later assigned to Jonai in the Dhemaji district to begin pastoral and educational work in the region.

Prior to the arrival of the Missionaries of St. Francis de Sales (MSFS), Catholics had already been present in Jonai for nearly two years. These early Catholic settlers were mainly Garo families who had migrated for work to the land of the Adi community of Arunachal Pradesh. Recognizing the need for spiritual unity, Fr. Abraham Kuttiankal brought these families together and began conducting Sunday services and prayer gatherings.

Over time, the Catholic population of the area grew into an organized community, and regular Sunday Mass was established. In the middle of 1979, a significant milestone was achieved when Fr. Cherian Moolamattom, the Vicar General of the Tezpur Diocese, was invited to bless and inaugurate the newly constructed school building. The Sub-Divisional Officer attended the event as the Chief Guest.

Despite being established midway through the academic year, the school began its journey with 65 students enrolled in Lower Kindergarten (LKG). Among the first students were Khogen Mardi and Dubai Soren. The first teaching staff of the school included Lucy Touro, Lucy Emmanuel, and Alice, who laid the foundation of academic excellence and discipline.

Fr. Abraham Kuttiankal served the mission faithfully until 1983, contributing significantly to its early growth and stability. Between 1990 and 1995, the school underwent major development with the extension of classrooms and the creation of a playground, addressing the increasing needs of students.

To further support academic life, a full-fledged bookstall was established for the benefit of both students and staff. Sr. Theresa Kurian, while serving as the Superior, took up the responsibility of managing the bookstall with dedication.
